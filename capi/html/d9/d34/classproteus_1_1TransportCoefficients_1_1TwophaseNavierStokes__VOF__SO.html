Detailed Description

Two-phase Navier-Stokes equations (volume-of-fluid formulation)

The equations are formulated as

\begin{eqnarray*} \nabla \cdot \mathbf{v} &=& 0 \\ \frac{\partial \mathbf{v} }{\partial t} + \nabla \cdot \left(\mathbf{v}\otimes \mathbf{v} - \nu \Delta \mathbf{f} \right) +\frac{\nabla p}{\rho} - \mathbf{g} &=& 0 \end{eqnarray*}

where the coefficients are given by

\begin{eqnarray*} \rho &=& \rho_0 (1-V) + \rho_1 V \\ \nu &=& \nu_0 (1-V) + \nu_1 V \\ \end{eqnarray*}

where \(V\) is the fraction of space occupied by the fluid, which is provided from some other model that is solved seperately (via operator splitting). 

The coefficients for two incompresslble fluids governed by the Navier-Stokes equations and separated by a sharp interface represented by a volume of  fluid (volume fraction) function
